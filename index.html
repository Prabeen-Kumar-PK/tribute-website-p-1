Missile Man of India

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, also known as the "Missile Man of India," was a renowned scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam's contributions to the field of science and technology are immense. He was the chief architect of India's missile program and played a crucial role in the development of the country's space program.

Dr. Kalam was also an inspirational figure and a prolific writer, who authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds." He was a humble and kind person who always encouraged young people to pursue their dreams and goals. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire generations.

Tragedy king of Indian Film

Dilip Kumar was one of the greatest actors in the history of Indian cinema. Born as Yusuf Khan in Peshawar, Pakistan, on December 11, 1922, Dilip Kumar made his acting debut in 1944 with the film "Jwar Bhata." He went on to act in several iconic films such as "Mughal-e-Azam," "Devdas," and "Naya Daur," among others.

Dilip Kumar was known for his natural acting style, powerful dialogue delivery, and ability to convey a range of emotions through his performances. He was also a recipient of several awards, including the Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan, and the Dadasaheb Phalke Award, India's highest award for contribution to Indian cinema. Dilip Kumar passed away on July 7, 2021, leaving behind a rich legacy in Indian cinema.
